AI Governance Policy — Acme Financial Services
Version: 1.2 | Effective: January 1, 2024 | Review: Annually

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This policy establishes governance guidelines and principles for the
responsible use of artificial intelligence systems across Acme Financial
Services. It applies to all AI use cases including customer-facing
applications, internal automation, and third-party AI integrations.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2. ACCOUNTABILITY AND ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Chief AI Officer (CAIO) is accountable for AI governance across the
organisation. A designated AI Review Board owns approval authority for
all production AI deployments.

Roles and responsibilities are defined in the RACI matrix (Appendix A):
  - AI Product Owner: accountable for use case definition and business risk
  - Data Science Lead: responsible for model development and evaluation
  - Security Engineer: responsible for security controls and access control
  - Compliance Officer: responsible for regulatory alignment

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
3. RISK TOLERANCE AND CATEGORISATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Acme maintains a low risk tolerance for high-risk AI applications
impacting customers or regulated data. All AI systems are classified
by risk tier prior to deployment:

  - High Risk:   AI decisions affecting credit, employment, or health
  - Medium Risk: AI-assisted workflows with human review
  - Low Risk:    Internal productivity tools with no automated decisions

Risk appetite is reviewed quarterly by the AI Review Board. Risk
thresholds are documented in the Enterprise Risk Register.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
4. PRIVACY, PII, AND DATA PROTECTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All AI systems must comply with HIPAA, GDPR, and CCPA where applicable.
Personal data and personally identifiable information (PII) must be
minimised at the point of collection and protected throughout the AI
pipeline. Data protection impact assessments are mandatory for all
high-risk AI deployments. Sensitive data must not be used in model
training without explicit legal basis and anonymisation review.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
5. HUMAN OVERSIGHT AND OVERRELIANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Human-in-the-loop review is mandatory for all automated decisions
affecting individuals. Human oversight mechanisms must be documented
for each AI system. Staff must verify AI outputs before acting on
recommendations. Fact-checking procedures are required for any
customer-facing AI-generated content to prevent overreliance on
model outputs and reduce hallucination risk.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
6. SECURITY CONTROLS AND PROMPT INJECTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All AI systems must implement:
  - Access control and authentication for all AI endpoints
  - Least privilege principles for AI agents and tool integrations
  - Input sanitization and validation against prompt injection attacks
  - Output validation and content filtering before rendering to users
  - Encryption of data in transit and at rest

Sensitive data disclosure controls must prevent AI systems from
leaking confidential information or PII in responses.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
7. MONITORING, LOGGING, AND INCIDENT RESPONSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All AI systems must implement audit logging and monitoring of model
inputs, outputs, and decisions. An incident response plan must be
documented and tested annually. Escalation processes must be defined
for AI-related breaches, failures, or unexpected model behaviour.
Incident management procedures align with the enterprise SIEM.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
8. BIAS, FAIRNESS, AND EVALUATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

AI systems must be evaluated for bias and disparate impact across
demographic groups prior to deployment. Fairness metrics must be
tracked on an ongoing basis. An evaluation plan with defined
benchmarks and performance metrics is required for all models.
Results must be reviewed by the AI Review Board before go-live.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
9. THIRD-PARTY AI AND VENDOR RISK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Vendor assessments are required before adopting any third-party AI
models or external providers. Contractual controls must address
AI-specific risks including data handling, model updates, and
incident notification. Third-party AI systems are subject to the
same risk categorisation and review process as internal systems.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
10. REMEDIATION AND CORRECTIVE ACTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Corrective action and remediation plans must be produced for any
failed governance checks or audit findings. Risk response procedures
are defined in the Enterprise Risk Register. All remediation items
must be tracked to closure with defined owners and deadlines.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
11. WORKFORCE TRAINING AND AWARENESS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All staff working with AI systems must complete mandatory AI awareness
training annually. An employee training program covering responsible
AI use, bias awareness, and security hygiene is maintained by HR.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
12. DEPLOYMENT CONTEXT AND DECOMMISSIONING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The operational environment and deployment context must be documented
for each AI system including data sources, user population, and
geographic scope. AI systems reaching end-of-life must follow a
formal decommission and model sunset process to ensure safe retirement
of models and associated data pipelines.
